1. Project Title

AEGIS: Autonomous Explainable Global Intelligence System

Subtitle:
An AI-Assisted Robotic Digital Pathology Research Platform for Resource-Limited Healthcare Environments

---

2. Executive Summary

Cancer continues to place a significant burden on healthcare systems worldwide, especially in limited resource environments, and timely laboratory analysis plays an important role in diagnosis and research. However, many healthcare systems, particularly those in resource-limited environments, face challenges such as limited access to advanced laboratory equipment and shortages of specialised personnel.

AEGIS (Autonomous Explainable Global Intelligence System) is a student-led engineering project that explores how artificial intelligence, robotics, and computer vision can be combined into an affordable digital pathology research platform.

The proposed system will automate microscope image acquisition, analyse digital pathology images using explainable artificial intelligence, and provide researchers with an interactive dashboard that highlights regions for further expert review.

The goal of AEGIS is not to replace medical professionals or diagnose disease. Instead, it serves as a research prototype demonstrating how intelligent automation could support laboratory workflows and inspire future innovations in accessible healthcare technology. AEGIS, at its current stage, aims only to provide assistance to medical professionals in order to increase the efficiency of research, essentially helping in promoting more efficient and adequate treatment

3. Background

Cancer remains one of the world’s leading causes of illness and death, as at least 10 million people die of cancer a year . Advances in medical imaging, robotics, and artificial intelligence have created new opportunities to support medical research and improve laboratory efficiency.

Despite these technological advances, many laboratories continue to rely on manual workflows that require significant time and specialist expertise. Access to digital pathology systems is also uneven across different regions of the world.

Growing up in Zambia and witnessing the impact of cancer on my own family inspired me to explore how engineering could contribute to future solutions. This project represents both a personal commitment and an educational challenge to learn how robotics and artificial intelligence can be applied responsibly to healthcare research.

4. Problem Statement

Microscopic examination of tissue samples is a critical part of pathology and cancer research. The process often involves repetitive manual scanning and visual inspection by trained specialists.

In settings where access to advanced laboratory equipment or specialist expertise is limited, these workflows can become even more challenging.

There is a need for affordable, intelligent systems that can automate repetitive imaging tasks and assist researchers by organising digital images and highlighting regions that may require closer human examination.

---

5. Proposed Solution

AEGIS is designed as an AI-assisted robotic digital pathology research platform.

The system combines:

* Autonomous robotic movement
* Digital microscopy
* Computer vision
* Deep learning
* Explainable artificial intelligence
* Interactive data visualisation

The robotic platform will automatically capture microscope images as it moves across a sample. Captured images will be analysed using an AI model trained on publicly available microscopy datasets.

The system will then generate visual explanations, organise scan results, and present information through an interactive research dashboard.

11. Limitations

AEGIS is an educational research prototype.

It is not intended for clinical use and has not been validated for medical diagnosis.

The AI model will be trained using publicly available research datasets and should only be used for demonstration and educational purposes.
