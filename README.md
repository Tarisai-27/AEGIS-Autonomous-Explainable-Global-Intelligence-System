# AEGIS-Autonomous-Explainable-Global-Intelligence-System

## AEGIS
# Autonomous Explainable Global Intelligence System

AI-Assisted Robotic Digital Pathology Research Platform
“Engineering intelligent robotic systems to support pathology research and improve access to advanced laboratory technologies in resource-limited healthcare environments.”

# Executive Summary
AEGIS combines artificial intelligence and robotics to explore how autonomous imaging systems and explainable machine learning can support and improve digital pathology workflows by addressing key diagnostic challenges.
The project combines robotics, computer vision, deep learning, automation, and data visualisation into a single research platform capable of:
Automated microscope slide scanning
AI-assisted image analysis
Explainable artificial intelligence (XAI)
Digital pathology visualization
Interactive research dashboards
Automated laboratory documentation
AEGIS is a student-led-engineering and research initiative inspired by the need for more accessible healthcare technologies, particularly in resource-limited environments

# Problem Statement
Cancer remains one of the leading causes of death globally, with 10 million people dying every year. Early detection often improves treatment options, yet many healthcare systems face significant challenges, including:
Limited access to pathology specialists
Manual and time-consuming slide analysis
High costs of advanced laboratory equipment
Limited access to digital pathology technologies in many regions
These challenges can contribute to delays in diagnosis and treatment.

# The Vision
AEGIS was designed to investigate how affordable robotics and explainable AI could contribute to and assist laboratory workflows by automating repetitive tasks, imaging tasks, and helping researchers identify key regions of interest.
The long-term vision is to contribute towards accessible digital pathology technology for hospitals and research initiatives globally.
Project Objectives
# Robotics
Develop an autonomous robotic platform capable of:
Precise X-Y microscope slide positioning
Automated sample scanning
Intelligent movement optimization
High-resolution image acquisition

# Artificial Intelligence
Develop computer vision models capable of:
Tissue image classification
Detection of visually abnormal regions
Confidence estimation
Explainable predictions using Grad-CAM

# Human-Centered Design
Create tools that:
Assist researchers
Support pathologists
Improve laboratory efficiency
AEGIS is designed to augment human expertise rather than replace medical professionals.

Any AI-generated outputs are intended solely for research and demonstration purposes.
